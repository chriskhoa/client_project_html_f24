Health & Wellness

Overview

The University of Michigan is dedicated to supporting students' health and wellness through a variety of key resources. University Health Service (UHS) offers comprehensive medical care and health education to address students' physical health needs. Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) provides individual and group counseling, crisis intervention, and workshops to support mental health and emotional well-being. The Maize and Blue Cupboard ensures students have access to nutritious food by offering free groceries and essential items. Additionally, Wolverine Wellness promotes healthy lifestyles through wellness coaching and prevention programs, while the Recreational Sports program encourages physical activity with fitness classes and intramural sports. Together, these resources create a supportive environment that helps students thrive both academically and personally.

Resources for health and wellness

University Health Service

University Health Service (UHS) at the University of Michigan is a comprehensive healthcare facility dedicated to the well-being of students, faculty, and staff. UHS offers a wide range of medical services, including primary care, women's health, immunizations, and travel health consultations. Additionally, UHS provides mental health support through counseling and psychiatric services, as well as extensive health education and wellness programs that promote a holistic approach to healthcare. The facility is staffed by experienced healthcare professionals committed to delivering high-quality, patient-centered care. UHS also features a pharmacy, laboratory, and radiology services, making it a convenient one-stop shop for most healthcare needs. By prioritizing accessibility and quality, UHS plays a crucial role in maintaining the health and wellness of the university community. For more information, visit the UHS website.

Counseling and Psychological Services

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) at the University of Michigan is a vital resource dedicated to supporting the mental and emotional well-being of students. CAPS offers a variety of services, including individual and group counseling, crisis intervention, workshops, and psychoeducational programs designed to address a wide range of mental health concerns. With a team of licensed professionals, CAPS provides confidential and culturally sensitive support tailored to the unique needs of each student. Additionally, CAPS offers specialized services for issues such as stress, anxiety, depression, relationship problems, and academic pressures. By promoting mental health awareness and providing accessible care, CAPS aims to create a supportive environment that enhances students' overall well-being and academic success. For more information, visit the CAPS website.

Maize & Blue Cupboard

The Maize and Blue Cupboard at the University of Michigan is an essential resource dedicated to combating food insecurity among students. Located on campus, this pantry provides free access to nutritious groceries and essential items, ensuring that all students have the necessary resources to maintain a healthy diet. The cupboard offers a diverse selection of fresh produce, dairy products, canned goods, and personal care items, all donated by community members and local organizations. Operated by a team of dedicated staff and volunteers, the Maize and Blue Cupboard also conducts educational workshops on topics such as budgeting, meal planning, and nutrition. By addressing food insecurity and promoting healthy living, the Maize and Blue Cupboard plays a crucial role in supporting the overall well-being and academic success of University of Michigan students. For more information, visit the Maize and Blue Cupboard website.
